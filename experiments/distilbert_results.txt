Experiment 3: DistilBERT under Weak Shortcut Conditions

Clean accuracy: 0.855  
Flipped accuracy: 0.86  

Observation:
DistilBERT achieves similar performance on both clean and flipped test sets, suggesting that it does not rely strongly on the injected shortcut in this setting.

However, it is important to note that the shortcut signal in this experiment is relatively weak and not strongly correlated with the label. As a result, the model is able to rely primarily on semantic features within the text.

This behaviour differs from the results observed in the main experiment, where stronger shortcut correlations lead to significant performance degradation. This indicates that DistilBERT’s apparent robustness is conditional on the strength of the spurious signal, rather than an inherent resistance to shortcut learning.
