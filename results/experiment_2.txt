Experiment 2: Effect of shortcut strength

As the probability of injecting the shortcut increases, the model’s accuracy on the clean test set improves slightly, indicating that the shortcut provides additional predictive signal during training.

However, under the flipped condition, where the shortcut becomes misleading, the model consistently suffers a drop in performance. This demonstrates that the model relies on the shortcut to some extent, although it still retains the ability to extract meaningful semantic information from the text.

The relatively stable gap between clean and flipped performance suggests that the model exhibits partial but not complete dependence on spurious correlations.
